Insurance and Security Tips for Motorbike Renters

2026-01-20 · Abhinash Sharma

Renting a motorbike offers convenience and adventure, but ensuring proper insurance and security is essential for a hassle-free experience. Here are some crucial tips for renters:

  1. Understand Insurance Coverage

Check Inclusions: Always verify the type of insurance provided by the rental company. Basic coverage usually includes third-party liability, but it’s better to opt for comprehensive coverage that protects against theft, damage, and personal injury.

Ask for Add-Ons: Consider add-ons like personal accident insurance or zero-depreciation coverage for enhanced protection.

Read the Policy Details: Understand the deductible amount, exclusions, and the procedure for claims.

  1. Inspect the Bike Before Renting

Conduct a thorough inspection of the bike for any pre-existing damage. Document scratches or dents with photos or videos and share them with the rental company to avoid disputes later.

Ensure the bike has all necessary documents, including insurance papers, registration certificate, and pollution under control (PUC) certificate.

  1. Prioritize Safety

Always wear a helmet, even for short distances. Many rental companies provide helmets, but carrying your own ensures proper fit and hygiene.

Use locks or chains to secure the bike when parked, especially in crowded or unfamiliar areas. Consider GPS tracking devices for added safety.

  1. Follow Traffic Rules

Abide by local traffic laws to avoid fines and insurance claim complications.

Avoid reckless riding, as insurance may not cover damages caused by negligence.

  1. Emergency Contact Information

Save the rental company’s emergency contact details for quick assistance in case of theft, accidents, or breakdowns.
